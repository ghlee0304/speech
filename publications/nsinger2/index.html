N-Singer2: Improving the naturalness

of synthetic singing voice for the ballad


Gyeong-Hoon Lee, Tae-Woo Kim, Hanbin Bae et al

{ghlee0304, ktw0114, mskang}@ncsoft.com


Abstract

     It is important to represent the characteristic of a singer well to generate a natural singing voice.
Especially, singing voices in the pop ballad style song have more dynamic and complicated components
related to singer's vocal technique, such as vibrato, breathy sound, and F0 fine fluctuations,
than children's song style voices. However, it is difficult to model a singing voice in pop ballad
because a content and singer identity are entangled with prosody information.
In the paper, we propose a system for modeling natural singing voices by disentangling singer-independent
and singer-dependent features with multi-singer system. The proposed system uses explicit features
to disentangle timbre and prosody components. In the variation predictor of our system,
for each input note pitch, we use a difference-based F0 to only model the vibrato and F0 fluctuations
related to the characteristic of singer. And we also predict the voiced range of singing voice
to prevent predicting pitch in the unvoiced range due to wrong duration of musical note.
In addition, we use data-augmentation method such as pitch shift to generate a singing voice with
a more stable prosody. Experimental results show that our system can generate a more natural singing voice
than the conventional system.
